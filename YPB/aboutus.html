About us

Get to know our organization!

Young Peace Brigades (YPB) is a grassroots, non-profit, volunteer peace organization that has been working since 2004 in urban and rural villages across Ghana.

By living and working directly with the communities we serve, we identify key areas of needed development and facilitate sustainable solutions. Our priority program areas of promoting and advocating for peace, conflict transformation and management whiles working with institutions and other civil society groups to prevent and counter violent extremism by promoting a rights based approach.

YPB also engages in improving health, education and economic empowerment through auxiliary programmes like peace for health, teach education for peace and peace for economic empowerment by a belief in a holistic approach to sustainable development.

Young Peace Brigades (YPB) is governed by an international Board of Directors and is staffed by a small team of employees and volunteers. Working with local and international volunteers, we make the most of every resource and donation to effect lasting, productive change.

Rashid Zuberu - Founder, Young Peace Brigades

Mr. Rashid Zuberu is a Youth, Peace and Security Expert and the founder of Young Peace Brigades.
Mr.Rashid develops comprehensive peace programme for communities affected by conflicts in West Africa using tools such as preventive diplomacy and prev entive deployment.
He explores related challenges, and attempts to distill best practices, and considers the impact of recent normative developments on conflict prevention in fragile societies. He is a trainer on conflict transformation, peacebuilding and human rights.
Mr. Rashid Zuberu also serves on UNOY Peacebuilders International Steering Group representing West and Central Africa. He was a leading part of the UNOY Peacebuilders Youth Advocacy Team (YAT) that led a successful Global civil society effort for the adoption of a United Nations Security Council Resolution scr2250 on Youth Peace and Security.
He has expertise in International relations, Development, Health, youth engagement and security issues. He has traveled extensively across the world and also lived and worked in East and West Africa as well as Canada.
Mr. Rashid Zuberu has served on numerous international speaking panels on the issue of youth, peace, security and International development. Over the years has facilitated workshops and trainings for youths around the world and from post conflict areas in Africa.

Yvonne Abrafi Manu – Coordinator, Empower Women Campaign

Ms. Yvonne Manu hold a bachelor of Education and Master of Education Guidance and Counseling from the University of Winneba, Ghana, She is a teacher by profession and has over 7 years of teaching experience with the Ghana Education Service.

Farida Ayishatu Iddriss – Outreach and Communication Coordinator

Ms. Farida Ayishatu Iddriss has 7 years of experience in the non-profits sector and has a Bachelor of Arts degree in social work from the University of Ghana, Legon. Farida is a public policy analyst and has a background in communication from Ghana Institute of Journalism.
She is frequently asked of her opinion on social and political issues both on the print and electronic media in Ghana. Ms. Farida is also a swimming coach in Ghana accredited with FINA (Federation International de Natation)

Ms.Bilikis Giwah – Advocacy Coordinator

Bilikis Giwah Habib is passionate and a student of life. Has good communication skills, versatile, and analytically minded.
Ms Bilikis was the first National Service Person in a small village called Apemanim in the Ashanti Region, where she taught both the young and old, and served as a mentor to the children. Worked at the Ghana-India Kofi Annan Center of Excellence in ICT, as a research Assistant, Bilikis worked on the FlossInclude Proj ect; the project with its funding from the European Union was to advocate for the use, and the creation of awareness of the importance of Free and Open Source Software.
Also took part in Idlelo4 conference held in Accra in 2010. She currently blogs on the use of Free and Open Source Software and Social and Political Issues. Ms. Bilikis has a Bachelors’ in Philosophy with Political Science from the University of Ghana.

Azoska Simeone - Head of Joint Programmes

Azoska Simeone is the Head of Joint Programmes Young Peace Brigades West and Central Africa. A Youth Peace and Security expert.
He was lead coordinator for the Young Peace Brigades and Global Youth Action Network for implementing the joint YPB/GYAN model Youth Early Warning Mechanism to check against tendencies of Violent Extremism among Youths migrating from conflict areas in the sub region.
Has a Master of Science in Defence and International Politics from the Ghana Armed Forces Command and Staff College where he is investigating the contestations around the Responsibility to Protect Norm and Power politics within the context of Human Security. Over the years has facilitated workshops and trainings for youths from post conflict areas in Africa.
He’s passionate about human solutions to ending conflicts. He has worked with the National Youth Authority in Ghana, founded the Peace Secretariat at the Kwame Nkrumah University of Science and Technology, was part of the expert group for the common Africa position of the post 2015 agenda by UNECA, Africa Development Bank and the African Union.
Founded the UN Youth delegate programme in Ghana and was Ghana’s youth delegation leader for the 64th session. He has implemented projects funded by the World BANK, British Council, UNFPA, Government of Ghana and many others. He’s a creative thinker and idea generator who believes in promotion of liberal norms that propel globalization. He has made enormous contributions to several initiatives in Ghana, Africa and the rest of the world.

Growing the Local Economy through Initiatives and our Service Learning

We believe that with all this initiatives we build a better Ghana

Promoting Tourism

There is a steady stream of tourists to Salaga in the Northern Region who visits the slave markets, slave wells and other historic sites to climb the peak, however, the majority of those tourists travel past Salaga to Tamale the regional capital that dominate the local tourism industry.
We are also providing start-up training to the youth and locals in breadbasket weaving, shea-butter production and our hopes is to establish sustainable economic hub for the local economy.

Growing the Crafting Industry

Y.P.B encourages home businesses where families could make quality items available for sale locally.
​We provide continuous support to the women that currently make the eco-weave bags and promote and sell those bags.  However we are looking to diversify the items made and sold.  If you have a skill set you are willing to share that could be a potential home-based business for members of our community please get in touch with us and consider volunteering.

Environmental Initiatives and Recycling

The beauty of our region is breathtaking but upon a closer inspection one will see that many of our landscape are littered with trash.  YPB will be working to minimize our use of plastic and implementing composting and recycling programs in 2017 and plan to have community-wide recycling programs in place by end of 2017.

Service Learning Program

Young Peace Brigades Community Service Learning Program is critical to the local economy as volunteer program fees go directly to community members and to small businesses within the rural villages where we live and work.  Our volunteers stay with host families where they get their meals, local community members organize our excursions and community members provide all of our transportation within Ghana.  Outside of agriculture and Tourist site, YPB is the leading force in the local economy as the majority of our annual budget is reinvested in the communities where we work.  Our Ambassadors, Service Learning volunteers and Field Service participants support the economy further by spending money in the local shops, bars and restaurants.
